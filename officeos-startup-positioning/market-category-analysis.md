# Market Category Analysis: OfficeOS
*Skill: startup-positioning | Generated: 2026-06-10*

## Candidate Categories
### AI Mobile App Builder: Existing
Good for attention, bad for trust. Buyers will compare OfficeOS to prompt-based tools and ask why they cannot do it themselves.

### Mobile App Development Agency: Existing
Easy to understand, but too broad and often associated with high price, slow cycles, and custom-project overhead.

### App Maintenance Service: Existing
Useful as a retention layer, weak as the main category. It starts the relationship at the hardest point: unknown code and inherited infrastructure.

### Managed Mobile App Creation: Subcategory
Best fit. It frames OfficeOS as outcome ownership for non-technical customers while leaving room for AI-assisted implementation, dashboard workflow, QA, release readiness, and recurring updates.

### Spec-Enforced App Studio: New/Subcategory
Differentiated but abstract. Better as product proof inside the dashboard than as the market category.

## Recommendation
Use **managed mobile app creation** as the category. The public phrase should be simple:

**OfficeOS builds updateable mobile apps for non-technical owners.**

## Implementation
- **Category label:** Managed mobile app creation.
- **Tagline direction:** App creation that does not end at launch.
- **Buyer expectation alignment:** Show app scope, launch deliverables, payments setup, QA, release readiness, and update plan.
- **What to avoid:** "AI app builder," "maintenance platform," "developer OS," "spec enforcement platform."

## Red Flags
- A public "maintain your app" sign-up can attract messy legacy apps that destroy margins.
- A public "AI app builder" sign-up can attract low-willingness-to-pay users who expect instant generation.

## Yellow Flags
- "Managed" implies human service, so delivery capacity must match promises.
- The offer needs visible examples of what is included and excluded.
