|---|---|---|---|---|
| AI app generation is mainstreaming | Technology | [Data] 2026 app-builder comparisons list many AI/no-code tools competing on prompt-to-app, native support, and code export. | Strong | Do not compete as another generator. | Mobile release remains operationally complex | Behavioral | [Data] Release management content describes the coordination required to ship app updates. | Moderate | Human verification and release ownership are credible differentiators. |
| Self-serve buyer expectation is rising | Behavioral | [Assumption] AI tools train buyers to expect dashboards and instant starts. | Moderate | A public sign-up flow is useful, but the offer should still gate scope before promising delivery. |

## Timing Assessment
The timing favors a hybrid category: AI has made buyers believe apps should be cheaper and faster, but that also makes pure app generation feel commoditized. OfficeOS should use AI internally and expose a dashboard, while selling the part customers still cannot trust themselves: scoped build, release readiness, and future update control.
