# Alternative Mapping: OfficeOS
*Generated: 2026-06-10*

## The Job To Be Done
The customer is hiring a solution to turn an app idea or requested app change into a working mobile release without personally managing product scope, code, QA, App Store steps, subscriptions, analytics, and future updates.

## Direct And Adjacent Alternatives
| Alternative | Type | Hired For | Strengths | Limitations | Switching Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| AI/vibe coding tools | Adjacent tool | Quickly generate app code or prototype | Fast, cheap, exciting | Leaves production ownership, QA, app-store readiness, payments, and maintenance to the customer | Generated app is impressive but not safely shippable |
| No-code/low-code builders | Direct-ish tool | Build app screens and workflows without code | Lower cost, visual control, templates | Tool lock-in, platform limits, still requires builder skill and publishing knowledge | Customer outgrows builder or needs native/mobile-specific behavior |
| Freelancers | Agency/outsource | Build or change app work on demand | Flexible, familiar, often cheaper than agencies | Requires briefing, review, coordination, trust, and continuity management | Customer gets tired of chasing or re-explaining |
| Agencies | Agency/outsource | Full app build or maintenance | Credibility, team depth, process | Expensive, slow for small updates, often over-scoped | Customer wants a smaller, faster, cheaper path |
| Original developer | Status quo vendor | Maintain the app they built | Knows the codebase | Availability and incentives may be poor; customer remains dependent | Developer disappears, delays, or becomes too expensive |
| Maintenance retainers | Direct service | Keep app alive after launch | Recurring support and OS compatibility | Usually reactive and technical; does not necessarily own product iteration | Customer wants actual feature movement, not just support |
| Doing nothing | Status quo | Avoid complexity and cost | No immediate effort | App idea never ships or app goes stale | Lost revenue, user complaints, campaign deadline, App Store issue |

## Key Insights
- Maintenance-only competes with low-trust retainers and original developers. It is valuable but hard to sell cold because the customer must grant access before OfficeOS proves competence.
- App creation has a clearer painful moment: "I want an app, but I do not want to manage developers." Maintenance becomes more defensible after OfficeOS owns the baseline architecture and release workflow.
- The strongest position is not "we maintain any app." It is "we build apps that come with an update system."
