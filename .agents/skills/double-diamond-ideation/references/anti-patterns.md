# Ideation Anti-Patterns

Use this reference when the user feels stuck, overthinks competitors, or cannot simplify a complex idea without feeling dishonest.

## Premature Convergence

**Pattern:** The assistant or founder jumps from a vague idea to one polished pitch too early.

**Why it fails:** The chosen answer may sound clean but is not grounded in a broad enough search.

**Correction:** Generate multiple audiences, painful moments, alternatives, and wedges before writing the pitch.

## Market-First Thinking

**Pattern:** Starting with "the app development market", "the creator economy", or "SMBs" before naming a painful moment.

**Why it fails:** Markets are too abstract to pitch.

**Correction:** Move from market to moment:

**Who is stuck, when, doing what, with which bad workaround?**

## Feature-First Thinking

**Pattern:** Explaining the system, architecture, workflow, or AI mechanism before the customer transformation.

**Why it fails:** The audience cannot evaluate mechanics until it understands why the result matters.

**Correction:** Lead with before/after:

**Before: [painful current state]. After: [desired state].**

## Competitor Anxiety

**Pattern:** "But agencies/freelancers/tools can also do this."

**Why it fails:** Almost every useful product has alternatives. The question is not whether alternatives exist; it is when they are bad enough.

**Correction:** Ask:

1. What does the customer use today?
2. Why is that alternative good in general?
3. Why is it bad in this specific context?
4. Which scope makes our advantage matter most?

## Edge-Case Capture

**Pattern:** Letting rare or complex exceptions define the main pitch.

**Why it fails:** The core story becomes a technical support document.

**Correction:** Sort details into:

1. Core.
2. Proof.
3. Scope.
4. FAQ.

## Weakening Instead Of Scoping

**Pattern:** Making the claim less specific because it is not true for everyone.

Weak:

**"We help with software work."**

Better:

**"We help non-technical app owners ship reliable mobile updates without managing developers."**

**Correction:** Do not weaken the claim first. Narrow the context first.

## Tool Bias

**Pattern:** Assuming the answer must be software because the founder can build software.

**Why it fails:** Early validation may require a service, spreadsheet, manual workflow, or concierge MVP.

**Correction:** During the second divergence, generate solution shapes across tool, service, marketplace, workflow, API, and expert network.

## One-Step Ideation

**Pattern:** Asking an LLM for "the best startup idea" or "the best niche".

**Why it fails:** It rewards confident synthesis without enough exploration.

**Correction:** Use the double diamond:

1. Diverge on problem space.
2. Converge on wedge.
3. Diverge on solution shapes.
4. Converge on concept and validation.
