# Scoring Rubric

Use this rubric when comparing niches, wedges, audiences, or solution shapes.

Score 1-5. A high total is useful, but do not blindly choose the highest total. Prefer the option that is sharpest, easiest to test, and most likely to create a strong initial wedge.

## Wedge Scoring

| Dimension | 1 | 3 | 5 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Pain intensity | Mild annoyance | Clear operational pain | Expensive, risky, urgent, or reputation-damaging |
| Frequency | Rare | Occasional | Repeated weekly/daily or tied to ongoing workflow |
| Reachability | Hard to identify or contact | Reachable through some channels | Easy to find via communities, platforms, lists, or direct access |
| Budget | No clear spend | Some indirect spend | Existing budget or strong willingness to pay |
| Alternative weakness | Current solution is fine | Current solution has visible gaps | Current solution is painfully broken for this context |
| Value clarity | Needs long explanation | Somewhat understandable | Obvious in one sentence |
| Scope control | Broad and edge-case heavy | Some boundaries | Narrow enough to make a strong truthful claim |
| Founder advantage | No special insight/access | Some relevant knowledge | Strong lived experience, access, credibility, or speed |

## Interpretation

Use these bands:

1. **32-40:** Strong candidate. Worth specifying and testing.
2. **24-31:** Plausible but needs sharper scope or validation.
3. **16-23:** Weak unless there is a hidden founder advantage.
4. **Below 16:** Usually too vague, low pain, or hard to reach.

## Tie-Breakers

When scores are close, prefer the candidate with:

1. A painful moment that can be described as a scene.
2. A clearly named current alternative.
3. A buyer or user who is easy to find.
4. A small test that can be run in days.
5. A claim that becomes stronger when scoped narrower.

## Red Flags

1. The audience is "everyone who..."
2. The pain is "saving time" with no specific painful moment.
3. The current alternative is unnamed.
4. The solution requires massive adoption before value exists.
5. The pitch depends on educating the customer on why the problem matters.
6. Most objections are actually scope problems.

## Green Flags

1. The customer already pays for an inferior workaround.
2. The problem repeats.
3. The user can describe the moment in plain language.
4. The first version can be concierge/manual.
5. The wedge is narrow but can expand later.
6. The idea gets easier to explain when made smaller.
