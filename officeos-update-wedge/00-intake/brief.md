# Intake Brief: officeos-update-wedge

*Skill: double-diamond-ideation | Generated: 2026-06-08*

## Starting Idea

OfficeOS was pitched as an accountable delivery layer for mobile apps: customers bring app direction, OfficeOS turns it into a tested, shipped, maintained app.

## Trigger / Founder Context

The hackathon post-mortem says the pitch lost clarity because it mixed three ideas:

- User feedback after launch.
- Apps needing continuous iteration.
- The mobile production lifecycle.

The founder tried to explain the whole system before choosing a narrow use case. The sharper idea in the post-mortem is updates: "spec in -> human quality update out."

## Known Audiences

- Non-technical founders with an existing app or clear app direction.
- Creators or small businesses whose app needs ongoing updates.
- Small teams that can describe product changes but do not want to manage mobile delivery.
- Agencies that maintain client apps and need a repeatable update workflow.

## Known Constraints

- The pitch must be judge-friendly and short.
- It should not explain all edge cases up front.
- It should follow the hackathon rubric: specific customer, real problem, product proof, AI leverage, founder edge, next step.
- The pitch should avoid generic "AI app builder" positioning.

## What Feels Unclear

- Whether OfficeOS is a first-build company, an app operations company, an agency replacement, or an update delivery layer.
- How to explain the business without needing a full architecture explanation.
- How to simplify truthfully without hiding hard mobile lifecycle details.

## Initial Assumptions

- The strongest initial wedge is post-launch updates, not first app creation.
- The buyer already has an app, a Figma/spec, or a clear requested change.
- The painful moment is recurring: "We need this app changed, but every change pulls us back into developer management."

## Open Questions

- Which initial segment is easiest to reach for paid pilots?
- How much manual operator work is acceptable in the first version?
