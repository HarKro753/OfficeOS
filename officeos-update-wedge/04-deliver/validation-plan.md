## Riskiest Assumptions

- Non-technical app owners feel enough pain around updates to pay now.
- They trust a managed operator-plus-AI workflow with their app.
- The first updates can be scoped narrowly enough to deliver profitably.
- "Human-verified update" is clearer and more compelling than "AI app delivery OS."

## Fastest Tests

- Manual concierge test: deliver 3 paid update pilots for app owners with existing apps.
- Cold outreach test: message founders and creators with "Need app updates without managing a developer?"
- Workflow teardown: interview app owners who recently delayed or abandoned an update.
- Fake door test: create a landing page for "Describe update -> verified mobile release" and measure qualified leads.
- Paid pilot: sell a fixed-price first update package before building more platform automation.

## Success Signals

- Owners can name a delayed update immediately.
- Owners understand the offer in one sentence.
- Owners are willing to share app access or source-of-truth files for a scoped pilot.
- At least one customer asks about recurring updates after the first delivery.

## Failure Signals

- Prospects only want a one-time first build.
- Prospects cannot provide clear update direction.
- App onboarding dominates the delivery time.
- The offer is compared only to cheap code generation rather than managed release ownership.
