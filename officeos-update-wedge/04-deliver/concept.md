## One-Liner

OfficeOS helps non-technical app owners ship reliable mobile updates without managing developers.

## Customer

Non-technical founders, creators, small teams, and app owners with an existing app or clear app direction.

## Painful Moment

They know the app needs a change after real users or clients expose the next requirement, but shipping that change safely pulls them into specs, developer coordination, QA, App Store readiness, and release management.

## Current Alternative

Hire a freelancer, keep paying an agency, rely on a technical co-founder, use AI coding tools alone, or let the app go stale.

## Broken Tradeoff

The owner must choose between fast but untrusted, trusted but expensive, cheap but unmanaged, or doing nothing.

## Product Promise

Describe the update once. OfficeOS turns it into accepted scope, implemented code, human-verified QA, and a release-ready mobile update.

## First Wedge

Small, recurring updates for apps that already have an app baseline and a clear owner: onboarding fixes, copy changes, feature tweaks, analytics events, paywall tests, design refinements, and release preparation.

## Not For Now

Blank-canvas app ideation, enterprise procurement, complex regulated workflows, unsupported legacy rebuilds, and "any app from any vague idea."

## Trust Mechanism

OfficeOS uses a source-of-truth workflow: change request, readiness gate, implementation, QA, release report, and updated baseline.

## Why Now

AI makes implementation faster, but faster code does not remove the need for product scope, QA, release readiness, App Store preparation, and update ownership.

## Main Risks

- Existing app onboarding may be too variable.
- Customers may need help writing clear update requests.
- Human verification must be priced so the business works.
