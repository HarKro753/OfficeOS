## Interpretation

The strongest candidate is human-verified updates for non-technical app owners. It is specific, painful, recurring, and easy to say without an architecture explanation.

The readiness gate scores well because it is narrow and founder-aligned, but it may be too small as the main pitch. It works better as proof inside the update workflow.

Agency maintenance is commercially plausible but less emotionally clear for a live hackathon pitch.

AI-code-to-production is timely, but risks sounding like a generic AI builder unless framed through updates and verification.

## Tie-Breakers

- Best painful scene: the app owner has a simple requested change but no safe way to ship it.
- Best current alternative: hire a developer or agency again.
- Best test: sell 3 paid update pilots to app owners with existing apps.
- Best founder edge: lived experience with mobile review, update, and App Store rejection cycles.

## Recommendation

Pitch OfficeOS as the update delivery layer for non-technical app owners:

**We help non-technical app owners ship reliable mobile updates without managing developers. They describe the change; OfficeOS turns it into a verified release.**

## Risks And Unknowns

- Onboarding arbitrary apps can create hidden complexity.
- Some customers may not have clear enough direction.
- Pricing must reflect real delivery cost.
- The pitch must make clear that human verification is a feature, not a lack of automation.
