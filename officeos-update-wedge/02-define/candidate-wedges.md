## Candidate 1: Human-Verified Updates For Non-Technical App Owners

**Customer:** Non-technical founders, creators, and small teams with an existing app or clear app direction.
**Painful moment:** They know what needs to change, but shipping the change safely means managing developers, QA, and release steps.
**Current alternative:** Freelancer, agency, technical co-founder, AI tool, or delaying the update.
**Broken tradeoff:** The owner must choose between speed, cost, control, and trust.
**Desired outcome:** Describe the update once and receive a tested mobile release plus an update report.
**Why this is urgent:** Apps need recurring changes after real users expose missing flows and new priorities.
**Why this is reachable:** Founders and creators can be found through founder communities, LinkedIn, app stores, and agency referrals.
**What is excluded for now:** Blank-canvas app ideation, complex enterprise apps, unsupported legacy codebases, and custom regulated workflows.
**Main risk:** Onboarding arbitrary existing apps may be operationally hard.

## Candidate 2: Agency Maintenance Back Office

**Customer:** Small agencies that maintain client mobile apps.
**Painful moment:** Small update requests consume senior developer attention and interrupt profitable new work.
**Current alternative:** Internal backlog, retainers, subcontractors.
**Broken tradeoff:** Agencies want recurring revenue but maintenance work is uneven and process-heavy.
**Desired outcome:** Turn client requests into verified releases with less internal coordination.
**Why this is urgent:** Maintenance requests repeat and client trust depends on responsiveness.
**Why this is reachable:** Agencies are identifiable and already buy delivery support.
**What is excluded for now:** Large agencies with mature internal delivery systems.
**Main risk:** Agencies may prefer white-label control and resist handing off accountability.

## Candidate 3: Mobile Release Readiness Gate

**Customer:** Small teams about to submit or update an app.
**Painful moment:** The team is not sure whether the release will pass review and behave correctly.
**Current alternative:** Manual QA, checklists, developer memory.
**Broken tradeoff:** Cheap to start, but limited to one stage of the lifecycle.
**Desired outcome:** Know whether the app is ready before submission.
**Why this is urgent:** Rejections and broken releases create delays and reputational damage.
**Why this is reachable:** App teams encounter this at every submission.
**What is excluded for now:** Full implementation and ongoing updates.
**Main risk:** Could become a checklist product with lower willingness to pay.

## Candidate 4: AI-Code-To-Production Mobile Delivery

**Customer:** AI-native founders who can create an app prototype but cannot operate production mobile delivery.
**Painful moment:** The generated app looks promising but cannot become a reliable mobile release.
**Current alternative:** Keep prompting, hire a developer, abandon mobile.
**Broken tradeoff:** Code is fast; production ownership is still hard.
**Desired outcome:** Turn AI-generated app direction into a shippable mobile baseline.
**Why this is urgent:** AI is creating more prototypes that need operational follow-through.
**Why this is reachable:** Builders are present in AI and founder communities.
**What is excluded for now:** Deeply custom native features and unclear product direction.
**Main risk:** May sound like another AI app builder unless scoped carefully.
